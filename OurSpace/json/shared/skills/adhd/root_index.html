Core filter

  • Every skill must have a 30-second version, a 2-minute version, and a full version.
  • Partial completion counts. Stopping before harm counts as success.
  • Use bed/chair-safe options first; standing, lifting, bending, scrubbing, and rushing are optional upgrades, never defaults.
  • Do not use shame, moral failure, laziness, cleanliness-as-worth, body blame, or 'just push through' language.
  • Build in caregiver capacity checks: 'Can we do this now, later, smaller, or not today?'
  • Avoid silent-treatment framing. A pause should mean: 'I am coming back; I am regulating.'
  • Protect privacy and safety. Do not expose addresses, medical details, trauma details, legal details, or identity history unless intentionally shared.
  • MCAS/respiratory-safe default: fragrance-free, low-dust, ventilated when appropriate, no harsh scents, and stop if breathing or allergic symptoms escalate.
  • TIPP/intense body skills need gentle alternatives because oxygen use, severe asthma, MCAS, EDS, vertigo, seizures, pain, and mobility limits may make cold shock, breath-holding, or intense exercise unsafe.
  • The app should invite, not command: 'try,' 'choose,' 'tiny version,' and 'restart' are preferred.
